Calendar Style — Font Pairing Options

Your baseline design is locked in. These samples vary only the fonts for your final decision.

Locked-in Baseline:
  • Rounded cells — border-radius: 4px
  • Gradient header — linear-gradient(135deg, monthColor, darker)
  • Larger cells — 2.5em
  • Drop shadow table — outer box-shadow
  • White cell backgrounds — #fff (safe for whisper mode)
  • Tinted weekday row — light tint of month color
  • Serif header / sans-serif body — month name serif, weekdays & numerals sans-serif
Roll20 constraint: Only web-safe/system fonts. No @font-face or Google Fonts in chat. Buttons always pink — cannot be styled.

B. Header Serif Font

Same baseline — only the month header font-family changes. All are web-safe.

B1. Georgia

Warm, round serifs. The most readable serif on screen. Very safe cross-platform.

safe bet

B2. Palatino Linotype

Elegant, calligraphic feel. Slightly more refined than Georgia. Good for fantasy settings.

elegant

B3. Times New Roman

Traditional, compact. Very familiar but can feel "default-y".

classic

B4. Book Antiqua

Similar to Palatino but slightly more old-world. Great for fantasy campaigns.

fantasy

B5. Garamond

Thin, classical. Beautiful but less reliable cross-platform (falls back to TNR on some systems).

risky fallback

B6. Cambria

Microsoft's modern serif. Clean, designed for screen. Windows/Mac safe.

modern

C. Body Sans-Serif Font

Georgia header locked. Only the weekday labels and day numerals font changes.

C1. System Stack

-apple-system, "Segoe UI", Roboto — matches OS native look. Clean and modern.

native

C2. Verdana

Wide letterforms, very readable at small sizes. The "safe bet" for grids and tables.

most readable

C3. Tahoma

Similar to Verdana but tighter spacing. Better for compact cells.

compact

C4. Trebuchet MS

Friendly, slightly quirky. Good personality without sacrificing readability.

personality

C5. Calibri

Modern, the Office default. Clean on Windows, falls back on Mac.

windows-biased
