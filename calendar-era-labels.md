# Era Labels and In-World Date Writing Conventions

This file captures practical era/date conventions for the calendars supported in this project.

---

## Eberron (Galifar)

## Era label

- **YK** — *Year of the Kingdom* (the Kingdom of Galifar).

## Typical written date

Common styles:

- `15 Sypheros 998 YK`
- `Sul, 15 Sypheros 998 YK` (weekday included)

## Typical spoken style

- “Sul, the fifteenth of Sypheros, nine ninety-eight YK.”
- Weekday is optional and often context-dependent.

## Notes for this script

- Current general formatter defaults to `Month Day, Year ERA` for non-Harptos systems.
- If stricter lore style is desired, a dedicated Eberron formatter can switch this to `Day Month Year ERA`.

---

## Faerûn (Harptos)

## Era label

- **DR** — *Dalereckoning*.

## Typical written date

Regular month days:

- `1st of Hammer, 1491 DR`
- `16th of Eleasis, 1491 DR`

Intercalary festival days:

- `Greengrass, 1491 DR`
- `Shieldmeet, 1492 DR` (leap year example)

## Typical spoken style

- “The sixteenth of Eleasis, fourteen ninety-one DR.”
- Festival dates are spoken by festival name directly.
- Weekday/tenday position is usually omitted in casual date statements unless specifically relevant.

## Notes for this script

- Harptos now uses a 10-day tenday display (`1st..10th`) for month table columns.
- Harptos date labels now follow `Nth of Month, Year DR` and festival-name handling.
